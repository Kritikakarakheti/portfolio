There are often two factors playing a critical role in the life of people with suicidal thoughts.

  1. Risk factor (Push factor)
    2. - Factors pushing them to commit suicide.
  2. Protective factor (Pull factor)
    3. - Factors stopping them from doing suicide till now.

The presence of pull and push factors is equal. That is the reason they are still alive. However, when things are difficult, they don't feel like considering their pull factors and instead put more emphasis on their push forces, which leads them to decide to kill themselves.

The nature of such people is determined by which factor is more active. More the push factors, the more chances to attempt suicide in life. So, examining the pull factors and making them aware of those is necessary.

It is crucial to determine the method of suicide and make an effort to eliminate it from the person's life, whether it be hanging, poisoning, cutting, taking an excessive amount of medicine, or self-burning.

At first,

  • Educating them on self-care techniques and distraction from false activities.
  • Persuading them that everyone, including physicians, experiences suicidal thoughts, thus they are not the only ones who do.
  • Making them aware of the importance of communication (expressing feelings rather than hiding)
  • Cultivating hobbies and self-exploration
  • Learning mindfulness (Suicidal thoughts arise when you are not mindful or run behind your emotions)
  • Observing the emotion and not chasing them (When you receive such thoughts, instead focus on your work or do breathing exercises)
  • Paying attention to your surroundings by feeling what you feel and seeing what you see.
  • Being mindful, being at present, and observing your thoughts
