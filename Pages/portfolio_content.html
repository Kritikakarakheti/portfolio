My Story behind AASHA

First scenario: I am down with a fever and cold. Maybe, it’s because you got completely drenched by rain yesterday. You should probably visit the doctor and take medicine. Second scenario: I am feeling depressed, lonely, restless, worthless, and hopeless. I don’t feel pleasure anymore. Why is it happening? What might have caused this situation? We would hardly get a genuine fulfilling comeback for this query. We barely find people in their real ecstatic state these days. No matter, how prosperous and able-bodied people they are, neither moneyed nor penniless individuals acquire a good state of mind. It’s nigh on impossible to find abundant mentality.

Regardless of the uncountable search, we scarcely encounter intrinsically elated people. According to the UN, the rate of depression and anxiety is rising by 25 percent in a year. This shows how drastically this issue is expanding on its own. Before evaluating how awfully the problem is widening, it’s always better to know the percentage by which it is accelerating, the major frequent cause, and things that could be done after getting aware of the cause. The above detail was all about the estimation from all over the world. However, In the case of Nepal, no strong survey and analysis are made up regarding mental health yet. The wonders of the human brain, turn us into real ecstatic during blithesome flashes and on the spur of the moment, switching us in the blink of an eye into the gloomy environment. Mood swings, behavioral control, emotions, understanding, and vision, these all factors used to excite me every time to learn exhaustively about them. The psychology of people recalling bad things more often in comparison to the good ones, audacity behind maturity and success, led curiosity in me to understand the brain, learn about human psychology, different mechanisms going on inside the brain, how it works, related to nerves, neurons, the transmission of signal and so forth. The data of the young generation driven towards sadness, anger issues, and easy excitability and slowly into a depression made me zealous and desirous to reveal better research and conclusion on it.

Still, people are unaware of where to go, and whom to consult, when they get unnaturally upset, depressed, and saddened apropos of nothing. No matter what might be the cause, depression in people impulsively leads them to have suicidal thoughts every time. The study shows depression rates soar more among college-age adults. To address this issue, I initiated the idea to conduct a research project regarding mental health. First, I along with my team started the project by taking a survey of secondary level and high school students on how they get affected by social media, how other people’s perspective matter to them, and how the negative sides of it create a problem for them. We also added up several questions related to getting the overall information about mental health issues. After the question-based survey, we evaluated the major cause and wrote a research report. Along with that, we tried to add every possible feature that might end up being helpful for people dealing with mental health issues in our app “AASHA NEPAL”.

After years of effort and research in the field of mental health, the project ‘AASHA’ was like a dream come true for me to establish an app and a website to mainstream mental health concerns in our community and serve as a reliable conduit where users can seek assistance.

While initially analyzing the services which are provided in mental health sectors in Nepal we hardly found any app or website which could by any means help an individual withstand their mental health problems. We read several research papers to precisely scrutinize the information regarding mental health in the country where we discovered numerous amazing findings. Among them, the one which truly intrigued us was the trend of people depending on faith healers (Dhami Jhakri). We found out that there were patients suffering from neurotic disorders, psychotic disorders, substance use disorders, and many more but seeking treatment from traditional faith healers. So why our project is unique? Firstly, there's no particular app in the play store which provides all the required features and necessary details regarding mental health services in Nepal. Although, our app may not contain each & every data in these 8 weeks though we gave our best effort so that we can bring even a bit more positive impact on people’s lives.
