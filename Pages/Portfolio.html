Imagine having a terrible day- a feeling of isolation, no motivation to move ahead, loads of pressure, but also procrastinating things again and again. Nothing comes up to your mind. You get the feeling to quit everything. But does that mean everything is over? For sure not, right?

HOPE (AASHA in Nepali)

The main reason many of us do not buckle under the weight of our circumstances is because of the hope that is in our hearts. To have hope is to want an outcome that makes your life better in some way. It not only can help make a tough present situation more bearable but also can eventually improve our lives because envisioning a better future motivates you to take the steps to make it happen.

Whether we think about it or not, hope is a part of everyone’s life. Everyone hopes for something. It’s an inherent part of being a human being while looking on the bright side and seeing challenges as opportunities where we say “Hoping for the best”

In this recent world where we are seeing more anxiety and depression in children of all ages. Tons of questions may arise in our minds.

Why are so many Teens depressed?

This serious mental health problem affects how a teenager thinks, feels, and behaves, and can cause emotional, functional, and physical problems.

Hence, with the intention to motivate people and sprinkle rays of hope in their life by stepping toward solving problems related to mental health, I am Kritika Karakheti from team AASHA, Incubate Nepal 2022 cohort.

When you experience difficulty, you have a choice. One option is to hide in a dark place away from the world, or you can face adversity head-on, knowing that you are not alone. You have us. You have AASHA.

AASHA Nepal App


At Incubate Nepal, an 8-week program with a 3.43% acceptance rate, I along with the team worked on the mental health initiative project known as Aasha Nepal under the guidance of our mentor who is a Data Scientist at the U.S Defense Health Agency. We built this app that acts as a reliable link between patients and professional therapists. To make sure we are on track, I monitored the team and assigned tasks. Our journey begins when we thought of building a database of professionals in mental health including psychologists and psychiatrists for our project. Later, Understanding the difficulty in finding professionals of our budget in our locality, we then added a feature of location tracking. In some adverse situations, motivational things might be our instant mood maker. Hence, we came up with the idea of other features like adding up sections for the motivational slider, transformation stories, notepads for drawing which is part of relaxation, and many more.

Mental Health Status of Middle Adolescents
(Age 12-19) - Research Report Analysis


As it is reported that almost all mental health issues start from age 14, I came up with the idea to conduct a survey through Google forms using a convenience sampling technique that aims to understand the mental health situation of adolescents and young adults ranging from age 12 to age 22.

Our survey involves around 900+ responses from all over the country covering nearly 43+ districts. While the survey does not specifically focus on specific stressors and mental issues, we analyzed the physical care habits, educational mindset, quality of social interaction, use of social media, and social stigma and their roles as stressors. Then, we prepared our research report based on that.

Fool Your Brain - Research Paper


After a year of research and in-depth conversations with 20 individuals who had dealt with mental health issues, I was motivated by the idea of placebo effects to write a research paper on the topic of "Fool Your Brain." It claims that we have the ability to trick or fool our brains in order to control our thoughts, behaviors, and brain activity.

1166 National Helpline for Suicide Prevention”- Led by WHO


I was the research associate for the 1166 National Helpline for Suicide Prevention project. I also created a report on "App Resource Demand" for making the product more user-friendly and successful based on the discussion I did with 20 patients by getting their consent and Mental Hospital approval.

It is a national-level project conducted by WHO where people with suicidal thoughts or emotional distress are encouraged to call 1166 (toll-free) and get 24-hour service.

This all started when we got the project regarding suicide prevention. The idea was initiated when they thought of modifying the system of helpline services related to the prevention of mental health problems and suicidal tendencies. Initially, the helpline service was running only through the helpline number directly linked to the counselor’s phone. While going through this way, several complications came up the way and it didn’t seem productive at all. Our team then thought of utterly switching it from this way to the path of digitalization.
