THE ART OF FOOLING OUR BRAIN

We frequently hear, taking a deep breath, attempting to smile more when life gets challenging or speaking to ourselves saying ‘Everything will be OK’ can fix some major mess in our life and can fool our brain into believing that we are happy.

So, does that mean, is it possible to physically modify our body in order to affect how we feel or think?

Since the body and mind are intertwined, how you breathe with your lungs may impact how you think and how your brain processes stress.

Deep breathing enhances your brain's oxygen intake and activates your parasympathetic nervous system, which helps you feel calmer. By focusing on your breathing, you may calm your mind and feel more at one with your body. This helps you feel grounded.

WHY DO WE FEEL MORE ALERT DURING THE DAY AND SLEEPIER AT NIGHT?

Keeping your eyes open is a method to become more alert. Eyes down is a shortcut to reduced alertness and sleepiness.

Your brain causes the production of the hormone melatonin, which induces sleep, at night when there is less light.

WHAT IS DOPAMINE DETOX?

Dopamine, a neurotransmitter that aids in regulating the brain's reward and pleasure regions, is said to have a role in sleep regulation, according to research printed in the journal PLOS Biology.

The pineal gland produces dopamine. Through the release of melatonin at night, which controls the body's metabolic activity while we sleep, this gland controls our internal clock (circadian rhythm).

The release of melatonin from the pineal gland and the circadian rhythm are both regulated by a different hormone called norepinephrine. Norepinephrine attaches to its receptors in cell membranes to perform its actions.

Norepinephrine

A hormone that influences mood, energy, and other things. It is a chemical messenger produced by your central nervous system and a stress hormone secreted from your adrenal gland.

It controls a variety of physiological processes to maintain the effectiveness of your body and brain.

Fool your Brain Mechanism

Your brain releases neuropeptides when you smile to help you cope with stress. Along with these neurotransmitters, endorphins, serotonin, and dopamine are also involved.

Serotonin is an antidepressant, whereas endorphin is a minor pain reliever.

As per one study, smiling can even lower our heart rates and speed up our recovery from stress.

Even trying to fake a smile and see how far that gets you can be worthwhile. There is some evidence to suggest that faking a smile might still improve your mood and level of happiness.

The human animal is programmed to be greedy. We are always tempted to do actions that will provide us with clear, immediate benefits in the short term, but will ultimately lead us in the wrong direction in the long run.

On the other side, we have the choice to do and act on those brightly colored things that seem as though they'll make everything better. The brain prefers to consume than to produce because it immediately releases dopamine.

Dopamine works just like our brain does when it evaluates everything in relation to the other possibilities we have on the table rather than objectively.

Power of the placebo effect

A placebo is when you expect wonderful things to happen to you and they occur. It has a positive impact on your body.

Nocebo means "I shall damage." You anticipate something horrible to occur at that time, and it happens.

They are both intriguing and challenging to study.

It is referred to as the placebo effect if the patient's condition improves while receiving the placebo treatment. The methods used to give the placebo and the patient’s expectations of the therapy both affect how well the placebo works.

Although it is possible to heal oneself mentally, the placebo effect is more complex than just happy thinking.

If you allow your mind the chance, it may be a potent tool for healing. The so-called placebo effect is the theory that your brain may fool your body into believing a phony therapy is effective. Science has now shown that, under the correct situations, a placebo may be equally as effective as conventional therapies. "The placebo effect involves more than just having faith that a therapy or procedure will be effective. It aims to improve the relationship between the brain and body and how they interact.

Even though the exact mechanism of action of placebos is still unclear, it involves a complex neurobiological response that includes everything from an increase in feel-good neurotransmitters like dopamine and endorphins to more activity in specific brain regions related to mood, emotional responses, and self-awareness.

The placebo effect allows the brain to communicate with the body about what it requires to feel better.
