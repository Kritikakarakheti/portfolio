Young Leaders Fellowship Report

My community issue was concerned with mental health including behavioral and emotional disorders, anxiety disorders, and depression among youths. It is a serious problem. Affected by several factors, youths get bizarrely upset, downhearted, and depressed inconsistently when the root cause of the problem is still undiscovered.

Most college-age students or young adults (14-24) are vulnerable to mental health-related issues. I believe, this age group is most likely to get into these types of problems since they easily get affected, sad and worried about life events. Some minor circumstances can also contribute to anxiety and depression. It’s very essential to find out the cause of the problem and solve it as soon as possible before it extends.

My mission statement was,
“Address the root cause of mental health-related problems and promote happy youths.”

My vision statement was,
“Creating the community where mental health is valued, promoted and protected, to attain high level of peace, love, and happiness all over.”

Report of Survey taken from 5 different individuals:

Survey No. 1

  1. What do you see as the most serious issue in the community affecting you?
    2. I feel like the issue of depression and anxiety is widely spreading among youths like me. Several social issues and distractions due to social media are extremely dangerous. I often get saddened and depressed while I use social media and begin to compare my life with other’s comfort zone. Sometimes, a small family issue can also harm mental health.
  2. Why do you see this as the most serious issue for you?
    3. I consider this issue as my biggest reason to feel demotivated most of the time. It always lags me behind resulting in extreme anger issues. I frequently lack confidence and prefer to stay alone avoiding conversations. Time and again, I get negative vibes with no self-assurance and the fear of failure.
  3. What do you believe is the best solution to the issue?
    4. At first, I think, we should change our negative mindset and build some positivity. Avoiding social media has somewhat helped me a lot throughout my mental health journey. It’s always essential to create awareness among people to follow their hearts and be themselves.
  4. How would you participate in the proposed solution (talk about the solution you proposed in the application)?
    5. As I said, I would love to create awareness among people on how essential it is to promote and protect mental health. I agree with the point of finding out the root cause of the problem before heading toward the solution. Last but not least, it is always good to listen to others.
  5. What do you do to escape from any kind of anxiety you suffer from?
    6. I would most likely talk with my close ones. In the worst case, I prefer sleeping.

Survey No. 2

  1. What do you see as the most serious issue in the community affecting you?
    2. The issue of getting distracted by the excessive use of social media is affecting me for some time. I feel like I am less worthy when I see people achieving higher than what I am doing. The anxiety about being able to determine the true passion for something and the career pressure is extremely high. Being an introvert, I feel lonely due to having fewer friends. I won’t be able to express myself.
  2. Why do you see this as the most serious issue for you?
    3. I get demotivated for not being able to excel in a certain field. Family pressure also occurs when you are affected by so many factors. I get the fear of not being able to cope with the situation. I also feel irritated and annoyed easily.
  3. What do you believe is the best solution to the issue?
    4. I think being self and not comparing can help. We must have self-reliance. For me, avoiding social media has also helped a lot. The idea of preparing a general routine and progress tracking can also help us to feel motivated and step the right moves. It’s very crucial these days to create mental health awareness.
  4. How would you participate in the proposed solution (talk about the solution you proposed in the application)?
    5. I would love to participate in this project by creating a group where everyone can open up their feelings, consult and help each other out.
  5. What do you do to escape from any kind of anxiety you suffer from?
    6. I tend to watch something entertaining. I prefer going in the fresh air. I talk to my close ones or my family. I eat something that makes me feel good.

Survey No. 3

  1. What do you see as the most serious issue in the community affecting you?
    2. I feel like a lack of proper communication is one of the serious issues. People are self-centric and just limited within a certain group.
  2. Why do you see this as the most serious issue for you?
    3. It has affected me so deeply. I used to feel isolated, depressed, and saddened. I used to feel difficult to interact with people.
  3. What do you believe is the best solution to the issue?
    4. I think being self and not comparing can help. We must have self-reliance. For me, avoiding social media has also helped a lot. The idea of preparing a general routine and progress tracking can also help us to feel motivated and step the right moves. It’s very crucial these days to create mental health awareness.
  4. What do you believe is the best solution to the issue?
    5. People should be open to discussion. Everyone should try giving a slight effort to support others. We should have the mentality of not letting anybody down. There should be freedom to speak.
  5. How would you participate in the proposed solution (talk about the solution you proposed in the application)?
    6. I would be more than happy to cooperate with the project by creating awareness among people to open up about their feelings. Organizing social gatherings and conducting games can also be a good idea. We can also organize an active participation program where everyone can interact and speak.
  6. What do you do to escape from any kind of anxiety you suffer from?
    7. Being an introvert, I usually don’t tell anyone about my feelings thinking nobody would understand. I sleep when I feel low. I tend to be hopeful for tomorrow.

Survey No. 4

  1. What do you see as the most serious issue in the community affecting you?
    2. The fact that mental illness isn’t considered with the same seriousness in lieu of physical illness.
  2. Why do you see this as the most serious issue for you?
    3. A stigma is rooted deeply in our society which labels and shames the victims of mental illness with obscene words and harassment. As much as the treatment of physical illness is crucial, mental health deserves the same, if not more caliber of priority.
  3. What do you believe is the best solution to the issue?
    4. The best solution begins with you and me advocating mental health in society itself because ultimately the change begins with us.
  4. How would you participate in the proposed solution (talk about the solution you proposed in the application)?
    5. Bringing up campaigns filled with volunteers and personally backed by Mohp and the government can be an incredible step for a start. Touring various communities, raising voices and such can perhaps cause an effective change in societal perception.
  5. What do you do to escape from any kind of anxiety you suffer from?
    6. In times of anxiety attack, a state of mind where the heart races to and fro, each section of the body incoherently panics and creates a chaotic disturbance. I often tend to stop whatever task on my watch and tend to practice slow breathing. After a decent state of control, I prefer consuming chocolate for instance.

Survey No. 5

  1. What do you see as the most serious issue in the community affecting you?
    2. Economic differences and Unhealthy competition seems to be the most serious issue adversely affecting me and youths like me. There is a huge rush to seek success in an inappropriate way to earn an ample amount of money.
  2. Why do you see this as the most serious issue for you?
    3. I believe every people won’t be able to gain maturity early in their lives. Several mental, physical, social, and psychological development happens with the process and the teen is very probable to get into these issues due to not being able to gain maturity and confidence at a certain age. They tend to attain success improperly due to which they often take wrong decisions and couldn’t able to return. We slowly develop insecure nature and disappear from our purpose in life.
  3. What do you believe is the best solution to the issue?
    4. There should always be an understanding among people. The process of developing a healthy competition should be adapted while being well-interactive and maintaining a friendly environment. We all should have healthy nature and proper counseling need to be there if anybody seeks help.
  4. How would you participate in the proposed solution (talk about the solution you proposed in the application)?
    5. Often parents don’t get aware and conscious of the child’s psychology due to which there is a high chance of children not being able to well interact with their parents. I would participate in the proposed solution by creating an awareness program and consulting parents.
  5. What do you do to escape from any kind of anxiety you suffer from?
    6. I pretend to be happy and show friendly nature to escape from my problem. I speak positively to acquire good vibes.

The conclusion from the report:

While taking a survey for a mental health project from 5 different individuals, each with their unique character, I observed some similar elements linking their responses.

For instance, after conversing with each individual, it was discovered that social media was one of the common elements among teenagers. People's real lives have been negatively impacted by their reel lives, either directly or indirectly. Youths are frequently demotivated and lack confidence as a result of losing their self-identity. They are readily swayed by other people's opinions and bear the dread to engage more easily. Following this survey to learn more about people's perspectives on mental health, I continued my project by conducting additional surveys from a variety of teenagers and college-age students to learn more about the common factors from which I can pick one to determine the root cause and work towards resolving it.
